## In Simple Terms

A baker takes an existing recipe, changes three ingredients, adjusts the temperature, and creates a signature bread. That is recipe editing.

A programmer takes an existing app, swaps the database layer, adjusts the UI framework, and ships a product for a different market. That is technology editing.

Patchtech is editorial technique applied to technology and materials. The word comes from "patch" (modify, fix, combine) + "tech." Every domain covered — software, hardware, IoT, mobile, commerce sourcing — follows the same pattern: take something that exists, deconstruct it, recombine it for your purpose.

This is not invention. This is not copying. This is patching — the technical form of editing.

## Patchtech is the Widest Skillset

Every other skillset has a clear domain boundary. EML is music. QSketch is drawing. MAL is language. PHL is concepts. PENON is diagrams.

Patchtech has no boundary. Any technology, any material, any system can be patched. The bakery example in the manifesto proves this — recipe editing is Patchtech applied to food. Fashion sourcing is Patchtech applied to commerce. IoT circuits are Patchtech applied to electronics.

This makes Patchtech both the most practical and the hardest to teach. The technique is universal ("deconstruct existing, recombine for your purpose") but the materials change completely across domains. The games address this by covering software, hardware, business, and automation — four entry points into the same editorial principle.
