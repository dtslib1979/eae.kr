## In Simple Terms

Most creators have one account, one voice, one audience. When they want to reach a different audience, they adjust their tone. Same person, different packaging.

Channeling technique does something different. Three Google accounts represent three genuine worldviews:

- Account A produces content that Account B would never make
- Account B asks questions that Account C would never think of
- Account C teaches in ways that Account A would find irrelevant

These are not personas in the marketing sense. They are actual perspective shifts. When you operate Account A, you think differently than when you operate Account B. The infrastructure (28 repos, CLAUDE.md, scripts) is identical — but the questions are different because the worldview is different.

  - Sync scripts transfer content regardless of the destination channel
  - The Matrix Architecture (ERP x FAB) applies universally

  What changes between accounts is not the system — it is the editorial judgment. Which content gets made, which questions get asked, which audience gets served.
  </AccordionItem>

  1. Close Account B terminal session
  2. Open Account C environment
  3. The questions change immediately — "How do I deploy?" becomes "How do I teach?"
  4. The same codebase produces different content
  5. Commit messages shift in tone and purpose

  This is not role-playing. The infrastructure forces genuine perspective shifts because each account has real audiences with real expectations.
  </AccordionItem>

## The Proof That Channeling Works

The three-account system is not a plan. It is already running. Real channels, real audiences, real content — produced from the same infrastructure but driven by genuinely different perspectives.

The evidence that channeling is real (not just role-playing) is in the output: Account A content and Account C content are fundamentally different in tone, purpose, and audience — yet both are produced by the same person using the same tools.

This is what distinguishes channeling from marketing personas. Marketing personas adjust packaging. Channeling adjusts the questions. Different questions produce different content, which attracts different audiences, which creates different revenue streams.

The process of maintaining three worldviews simultaneously — including the confusion, the perspective bleed, the moments where accounts produce surprisingly similar content — is itself broadcast material. The attempt is the curriculum.
