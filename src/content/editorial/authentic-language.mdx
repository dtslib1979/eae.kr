## In Simple Terms

Every AI-era language channel teaches you to use someone else's language better. Learn English. Master business writing. Copy the style of successful writers.

MAL asks the opposite question: **what is YOUR language?**

Not grammar. Not vocabulary size. Your language fingerprint — the specific word order you prefer, the sensory words you reach for, the rhythm of your sentences. Everyone has one. Most people never notice it.

MAL is editorial technique applied to language. The original material is the existing language system. You deconstruct abstract words into sensory experiences (temperature, weight, texture, color), then recombine them in your personal pattern. The result is not better writing — it is YOUR writing.
</Part1>

## System Architecture

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="1. Synesthesia Mapping">
  Abstract emotions get translated into physical sensations:

  | Emotion | Temperature | Texture | Weight | Color |
  |---------|-------------|---------|--------|-------|
  | Joy | Warm | Soft | Light | Yellow |
  | Sadness | Hot | Wet | Heavy | Gray |
  | Anger | Burning | Sharp | Solid | Red |
  | Fear | Cold | Hard | Lead-like | Dark |
  | Loneliness | Cool | Transparent | Absent | Clear |

  This is not metaphor. It is parameter mapping — same principle as EML's Plutchik-to-music matrix, applied to language.
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="2. Syntactic Fingerprint">
  Your language DNA can be measured:

  - **Word order preference**: do you front-load subjects or delay them?
  - **Adverb selection**: which modifiers do you reach for unconsciously?
  - **Sentence rhythm**: short-long-short? or building sequences?
  - **Preposition habits**: spatial metaphors vs temporal metaphors?

  Five sentences from you is enough to extract a fingerprint. The fingerprint is unique — like a writing voice that no AI can replicate because it was never trained on your specific pattern.
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="3. Han Kang Style Application">
  MAL studies Han Kang's technique as an editorial reference:

  - **Body-centered emotion words**: feelings described through physical sensation
  - **Present tense narration**: immediacy, especially effective in broadcasting
  - **Rhythmic sentence breaking**: short phrases that create reading rhythm

  This is not imitation. It is studying an original, deconstructing the technique, and recombining the principles with your own fingerprint.
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="4. Five Games + BOSS">
  1. SYNESTHESIA LAB — emotion word appears, set temperature/weight/color sliders, compare with others
  2. FINGERPRINT FINDER — write 5 sentences, AI extracts your syntactic DNA
  3. SENTENCE ALCHEMY — transform a plain sentence into sensory language
  4. SYNTAX DUEL — same content in two word orders, discover your thinking style
  5. LIVE LANGUAGE LAB — collaborative dictionary building during broadcast

  **BOSS**: Given 5 abstract concepts, write original + sensory sentence pairs. Scored on sensory word density, vocabulary diversity, and enrichment ratio.
  </AccordionItem>

## The Cross-Domain Connection

MAL shares the Plutchik emotion engine with EML and QSketch. One emotion, three outputs:

- EML: Joy 0.7 becomes G major at 132 BPM
- QSketch: Joy 0.7 becomes arched eyebrows and upward motion
- MAL: Joy 0.7 becomes "warm, light, soft, yellow"

A single broadcast episode can express one emotion through music, visual, and language simultaneously. This is why the six skillsets are not separate courses — they are different materials processed by the same editorial engine.
