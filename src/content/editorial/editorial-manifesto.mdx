## In Simple Terms

Imagine three people looking at a pile of LEGO bricks.

The first person copies someone else's build exactly. That is plagiarism.
The second person invents a brick that never existed. That is creation.
The third person takes existing bricks apart, picks the ones that matter, and builds something no one has seen before. That is editing.

EAE University teaches the third way. We call it **Editorial Technique** — the skill of deconstructing originals and recombining them into new meaning.

Six domains, one philosophy: EML (music), QSketch (drawing), MAL (language), PENON (diagrams), PHL (concept tokens), Patchtech (technology). All six are editorial technique applied to different materials.
</Part1>

## Beyond AI Declaration

Editorial technique is beyond AI because AI generates while editors curate. The distinction is not about quality — AI can produce excellent output. The distinction is about agency: who decides what matters, what gets cut, and what new context emerges from recombination.

The editor bears responsibility for the selection. AI cannot bear that responsibility. Therefore, editorial technique is the irreplaceable human function in content creation.

This is what EAE University teaches — not information (AI handles that), not skills in isolation, but the judgment to deconstruct and recombine across any domain.
