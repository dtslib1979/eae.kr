## In Simple Terms

Why can a character designer sketch a face in 10 seconds while you struggle for 10 minutes?

Not talent. **Unit libraries.** They have decomposed thousands of faces into reusable units — eye shapes, nose angles, jaw curves — and practiced each unit until it is automatic. Drawing fast means the units are already loaded.

QSketch is editorial technique applied to visual drawing. The original material is the real world. You deconstruct objects into geometric units, practice each unit in isolation, then recombine them into scenes at speed.

The editorial chain: hand stroke (original) to Samsung AI (first refinement) to DALL-E / Gemini (finishing) to human review (style ID check) to unit ID tagging to repository storage. Your hand stroke is the style ID — the irreplaceable original. AI is the finishing tool.

## System Architecture

  <AccordionItem title="1. Four Unit Libraries">
  | Library | Content | Key Technique |
  |---------|---------|--------------|
  | People | Face (emotion dashboard) + Body (verb units: REACH, HOLD, PUSH, PULL) | Physiognomy + Plutchik emotion mapping |

  <AccordionItem title="2. Face Design Formula">
  Faces in QSketch follow a structured formula:

  - **Physiognomy structure**: bone structure defines character
  - **French BD line language**: European comic tradition of expressive linework
  - **Plutchik emotion encoding**: same coordinates as EML, different output

  Joy 0.7 in EML = G major, 132 BPM.
  Joy 0.7 in QSketch = arched eyebrows + REACH posture + upward arrows.
  Same emotion engine, different material domain.
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="3. Pipeline — Liner to PSE">
  Two tools from parksy-image connect to QSketch:

  **Liner Engine** (XDoG parameters: sigma 0.5, k 1.6, epsilon 0.01, phi 10.0):
  Photo to sketch conversion. Produces clean linework from photographs.

  **PSE (Parksy Signature Engine)**:
  Handwriting SVG to TTF font builder. Each character's signature becomes a usable font. 86 glyphs built, Phase 2 awaiting real handwriting input.

  Pipeline: Liner sketch + S Pen handwriting on top + PSE converts to font = character gets a unique visual identity AND a unique typographic identity.
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="4. Five Games + BOSS">
  1. SPEED DRAW — unit name appears, sketch within time limit, AI scores similarity
  2. VERB MATCH — action sketch shown, select the correct verb
  3. EMOTION FACE BUILDER — physiognomy + emotion sliders generate face in real-time
  4. SCENE COMPOSER — drag unit cards to compose a scene
  5. LIVE SKETCH CHALLENGE — 30-second broadcast sketch duel

  **BOSS**: Given a philosophical axiom, draw it. 90 seconds. Scored on coverage, stroke quality, and conceptual accuracy.
  </AccordionItem>

## The Style ID Principle

AI can generate any image. But AI cannot generate YOUR style. Your hand stroke — the specific pressure, angle, speed, and imperfection of your line — is a biometric signature. It is the one thing that cannot be replicated by a model trained on everyone else's strokes.

QSketch preserves this principle rigorously. AI refines and finishes, but the original stroke is always human. The unit library approach ensures that your strokes become faster and more consistent over time, but they remain uniquely yours.

This is why speed matters: speed is proof that the units are internalized. A slow drawing means you are still computing. A fast drawing means you already know.
