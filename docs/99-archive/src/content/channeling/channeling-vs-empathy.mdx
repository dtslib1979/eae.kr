## In Simple Terms

Design thinking says: empathize with the user. Interview them. Observe them. Map their journey.

Channeling says: **become them.** Not metaphorically — operationally. Enter their perspective so completely that you ask their questions, not yours. Then hand those questions to AI from their viewpoint.

The difference:
- Empathy: "I understand your problem" (observer stays outside)
- Channeling: "I AM asking this question as you" (observer disappears)

Why does this matter? Because editorial technique, operational technique, and survival technique cannot cover every domain alone. You need other perspectives. Channeling is how a solo operator accesses expertise they do not possess — by entering someone else's frame and extracting what they would ask.

## System Architecture

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="1. The Channeling Pipeline">
  Step 1: Observe — study a real person (acquaintance, colleague, mentor)

## Channeling Feeds Survival

Channeling technique is not a standalone discipline. It is the input stage of a pipeline:

- Channeling (input): read people, extract perspectives, create character IP
- Survival (output): franchise the IP, replicate the system, build exit strategies

The two categories operate as a single pipeline. Channeling without survival is art. Survival without channeling is empty infrastructure. Together, they form the business engine of a solo operator.

The process of building this pipeline — including the failures, the wrong reads, the perspectives that did not produce useful questions — is itself broadcast content. The journey is the curriculum.
